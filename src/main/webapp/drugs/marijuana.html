Marijuana

Aliases: weed, pot, ganja, mary jane

Emergency Information

There have been no reported cases of people overdosing on marijuana alone and it is rarely laced with other drugs.
Signs of laced marijuana include:

  • Harsh Chemical Smell
  • The substance scratching surfaces when it is rubbed against them
  • Excessive amounts of powder/dust

In this case where you think marijuana you used was laced, seek medical attention immediately.

How it works:

Marijuana is a plant that grows naturally. It contains THC, a chemical that is responsible for the symptoms of using the drug.
These symptoms include:

  • Altered Emotions (including extended happiness and increased sense of humor)
  • Relaxation
  • Difficulty Problem Solving
  • Anxiety and Paranoia
  • Bloodshot Eyes
  • Dry Mouth, often referred to as "Cotton Mouth"
  • Increased Hunger, often referred to as "The Munchies"

Marijuana is most often smoked in its plant form.
It can also be turned into an oil and either be smoked using a vaporizer or be cooked into food and then eaten.

Where is it legal?

Marijuana is legal for recreational use in: Alaska, Arizona, California, Colorado, Washington D.C., Illinois, Maine, Massachusetts,
Michigan, Montana, Nevada, New Jersey, New York, Oregon, South Dakota, Vermont, Virginia, and Washington

Where is it decriminalized?

Marijuana is decreminalized in all above states as well as Connecticut, Delaware, Hawaii, Maryland, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri,
Nebraska, New Hampshire, New Mexico, North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, and Rhode Island
