Stimulant Overdose
What Is It And How Can You Help?
Stimulant overdoses occur when a person takes in too much of a given opiod. It can cause serious medical problems that can be lethal. An overdose happens when a person takes more than their body can tolerate, like when someone tries to take the same amount they did a previous time.
The signs and symptoms an stimulant overdose include:
• Excited
• High heart rate
• Fast speech
• Dry mouth
• Dilated pupils
• Sweating
• Spends long periods without sleep
• Restless
• Rigid limbs
• Seizures
Here are some ways to help someone who is experiencing an stimulant overdose:




• Put the victim in Recover Position.


• If they vommit, wipe and clean their mouth so they don't choke or inhale the vommit.


• Do not put the victim in a cold shower, it may put them in shock.


Recovery Position
Turn the victim on their side so they do not choke or inhale vomit.
It is important to call 911, as there is no antidote for a stimulant overdose. Medical professional will be able to care for the victim. If you believe the victim has become unconscious, click here.
