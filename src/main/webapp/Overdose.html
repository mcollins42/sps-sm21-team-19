Overdoses
What are they and why do they happen?
An overdose occurs when the body is contains too much of a poison. The poison can be anything from cleaning fluids, insecticides or carbon monoxide, and can be enter the body on purpose or accidentally. There are four ways a poison can enter the body:
• Ingested Poisons: poisons that are swallowed. Ex. medications or improperly stored foods.
• Inhaled Poisons: poisons that are breathed in. Ex. gases like carbon monoxide and ammonia.
• Absorbed Poisons: poisons that enter through the skin. Ex. insecticides and agricultural chemicals.
• Injected Poisons: poisons that are inserted through the skin. Ex. animal venom or drugs.
Overdoses can happen for many reasons, but usually happen by accident. A person could have accidentally eaten spoiled meat or touched an animal that produced a toxic substance. Either way, it is important to know what to do when overdose emergancies happen. On this page, we will discuss drug overdoses, how they happen, and what to do if someone you know has overdosed.
