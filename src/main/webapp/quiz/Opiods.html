Opiod Overdose
What Is It And How Can You Help?
Opiod overdoses occur when a person takes in too much of a given opiod. It can cause serious medical problems that can be lethal. An overdose happens when a person takes more than their body can tolerate, like when someone tries to take the same amount they did a previous time.
The signs and symptoms an opiod overdose include:
• Low heart rate
• Slow breathing rate
• Cooler skin temperature
• Constricted pupils
• Relaxed muscles
• Sweating
• Sleepy
• Respirtory or Cardiac arrest
Here are some ways to help someone who is experiencing an opiod overdose:
