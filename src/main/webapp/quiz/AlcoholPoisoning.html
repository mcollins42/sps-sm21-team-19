Alcohol Poisoning
What Is It And How Can You Help?
Alcohol abuse occurs when a person drinks too much alcohol. It can cause serious medical problems that can be lethal, especially if mixed with other depressants. An alcohol overdose, or poisoning, usually occurs after years of abuse and a persons tolerance becomes too high for their body to handle. However, it can also happen suddenly and often occurs at parties after a person drink too much too fast.
The signs and symptoms of alcohol poisoning include:
• Smell of alcohol on the person's breath
• Swaying movements
• Slurred and incoherrant speech
• Flushed face (pink, sweating, warm)
• Vomitting
• Poor corrdination and slow reaction time
• Hallucinations
• Lack of memory (blacked out)
• Altered mental status (can not correctly identify their name, location, or current year)
Here are some ways to help someone who is experiencing alcohol poisoning:


• Put the victim in Recover Position.


• If they vommit, wipe and clean their mouth so they don't choke or inhale the vommit.


• Do not put the victim in a cold shower, it may put them in shock.


Recovery Position
Turn the victim on their side so they do not choke or inhale vomit.
